MULTI-TASKING
IS A MYTH.

Here's the thing

You're not multi-tasking.
You're task-switching.
Badly.

Your brain cannot run two thinking tasks in parallel. It toggles. Drop one thread, pick up another, drop it, go back. Every toggle has a price.

Don't take my word for it

Let's prove it.
Right now.

The productivity illusion

Busy is not
the same thing
as productive.

Answering emails in a meeting isn't doing two things at once. It's doing two things badly at once. Switchers take longer, make more mistakes, and spend more time in transition than in actual work.

RUBINSTEIN, MEYER & EVANS — J. EXP. PSYCHOLOGY, 2001
And it's not free for your body

Your brain treats every ping like a small emergency.

Each switch releases cortisol and adrenaline. Useful for a bear attack. Terrible as an all-day baseline. That fried feeling at 5pm? That's not the workload. That's the switching.

LEVITIN — THE ORGANIZED MIND, 2014

So what actually works?

ONE THING
AT A TIME.

Focus isn't a personality trait. It's a system. Six moves, zero cost. The Focus Six.

The Focus Six — 01 & 02
FIX 01

The First 90

Your one thing happens before the inbox opens. First 90 minutes: one task, comms closed. Nothing in until the one thing is out.

FIX 02

Three touches

The average office worker checks email about 75 times a day. Check at 10, 1, and 4 instead. Same messages — 72 fewer switches.

The Focus Six — 03 & 04
FIX 03

Another room

Texas researchers found your phone drains your thinking even when it's off and face-down. Silent isn't enough. Off isn't enough. Another room.

FIX 04

Park it

Paper beside you. When the itch to switch hits — an idea, a worry, a "quick" search — write it down and stay. Open loops nag. The note closes the loop.

The Focus Six — 05 & 06
FIX 05

The two-sentence no

Exact words: "I want to give this real attention, and I can't today. Can I get you an answer by Thursday?" Protecting focus isn't rude. Half-listening is.

FIX 06

Tonight's sticky

Before you quit for the day, write tomorrow's ONE thing on a sticky. Keyboard, dashboard, toolbox. Morning-you spends the beam working, not choosing.

Your homework — tomorrow morning

One task. 90 minutes.
Phone in another room.

Not on silent. Not face-down. Another room. That's Fix 01 plus Fix 03 — nothing else. Then notice how much you got done, and how it felt.
